Bharatpur Beach

Bharatpur Beach is one of the most beautiful and lively beaches in the Andaman Islands, famous for its crystal-clear shallow waters and vibrant coral reefs. Located on Near Jeety Neil Kendra, Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep), it is an ideal spot for swimming, snorkeling, and glass-bottom boat rides, making it perfect for both beginners and families.

Laxmanpur Beach

Laxmanpur Beach is a serene and less crowded beach in the Andaman Islands, known for its pristine sands and calm waters. It offers a peaceful retreat for those seeking a tranquil environment away from the hustle and bustle of more popular tourist spots.

Natural Rock Formation

The natural rock formation in the Andaman Islands is a geological wonder, showcasing the unique landscape shaped by millions of years of erosion and tectonic activity. These formations offer a glimpse into the region's rich natural history and provide stunning photo opportunities for visitors.

Science centre

Port blair science center is one the best interesting science education center, where you can find the 3d show and many interesting thing like quiz corner fun science galley and sea plane etc. The Science Centre, situated five kms from Port Blair town, has an ‘Andaman and Nicobar Islands Gallery' with interactive exhibits depicting genesis of the islands, volcanic activities, science of oceans, fun science gallery, modern technology gallery, Taramandal (Portable planetarium), and an in-house butterfly corner. It is closed on all Mondays and Public Holidays.

Elephant Beach

Elephant Beach is a popular tourist destination located on Havelock Island, in the Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. It is known for its crystal clear waters and coral reefs, which make it a popular spot for snorkeling and scuba diving. The beach is also known for its elephant-shaped rock formation, which gives it its name. It's reachable on foot from Beach #7 or by fishing boat from the east side of the island; many resorts arrange snorkeling and diving trips here. A path to the beach starts from the road to Radhanagar — look for a dip in the road on a curve where there will usually be a few motorbikes parked. Several paths head off from this point, ask around for the correct one. If you choose to trek alone through the forest then stick to broad path since there are forks along the way.
